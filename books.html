Survivors of the Apocalypse

When the entire world tries to kill you, it's up to you to survive.

04-09-2025 130 pages N/A

What starts as a regular school day turns into a nightmare for sixteen-year-old Nino when the sky ignites and the world he knew vanishes in fire and silence. Now trapped with a handful of classmates—some friends, some strangers—in a scorched and unfamiliar wasteland, survival means more than just finding food and shelter. It means facing fear, grief, and the truths they've all been running from. As strange masked figures emerge from the ruins and whispers of something greater stir in the shadows, Nino and his group are forced to ask the hardest question of all: What if surviving the apocalypse was only the beginning?

Starting as a fictional story based on what would happen if I would wake up in a post-apocalyptic world, this book has grown into a fully fleshed out story, and seeing recent events across the world, it has become more relevant than ever. The book is an action packed, mystery filled adventure, with a lot of twists and turns. It is a story about survival, but also about the human condition, and how we deal with extreme situations.

Behind the Stories

My Writing Journey

I have always been told I have a vivid imagination, and I've been making up stories ever since I was a child. My first story, which I wrote at the age of 10, was about me and my family ending up in a strange world. It was simple, and had 5 pages, including the cover. But ever since then, I've been writing stories about scenarios that I would like to see. Like me and my secondary school class becoming the heroes of our school by defeating an alien monster (which is something I've written).

At school, I was one of the stranger kids, acting out my own stories in the playground, and I liked reading, which was something that not many of my classmates did. I was always fascinated by the stories I read, and I wanted to create my own worlds, with their own rules and characters. I started writing short stories, and eventually moved on to longer ones, and now I'm working on my first full-length novel.
